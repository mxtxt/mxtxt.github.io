The Themes

mxTXT will carry you in an adventure through history! From Gutenberg to the future of Aurora, you will witness attention-seeking Victorian playbills and explore the glamourous period of Art Deco, passing through the tropicalist jungles of Brazil and a London thrilled by the revolutionary style of The Face magazine.

mxTXT allows the visualisation of different documents through different historical periods' typographies and design. These periods span from the late Middle Ages, the 1400s, to the future, until 2040. The buttons on the right allow you to choose periods, and are organized on the page chronologically, from the past on the top towards the future on the bottom.

Theme 1: Gutenberg

Gutenberg’s Bible printed in 1454 was the main source of inspiration for this theme, which represents documents as they used to be set for their first printed versions in the XV century.

Theme 2: Victorian Playbill

Industrialization led to an abundance of products to buy, and with automated papermaking, paper became commonly affordable. Advertising boomed. Standing out as much as possible was the goal.

Theme 4: Tropicalia

A distinctly Brazilian take on pop art, Tropicalia was a counterpoint to the prevailing style of the military dictatorship. It informed every art medium, as it redefined the country’s culture, let down by modernity.

Theme 5: The Face

As an icon of style culture, this British magazine reflected the latest trends in art, design and fashion defined by the youth culture in the 1980s. The Face has been the main source of inspiration for printed texts between 1980 and 2000.
